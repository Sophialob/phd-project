Study Information Sheet

Coping and Help-seeking among Ukrainian Adolescent Girls and Young Women in the UK

This PhD project aims to explore coping and help-seeking among Ukrainian adolescent girls and young women (aged 15-24 years old) living in the United Kingdom (UK) since the onset of the Russia-Ukraine war in February 2022.

Why have I been asked to take part?

You are invited to participate because you are a Ukrainian adolescent girl or young woman who has moved to the UK since the onset of the Russia-Ukraine war.

Do I have to take part?

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You can withdraw at any time without giving a reason.

What will I have to do?

You will complete an online survey about your general health, coping strategies, and types of support you consider seeking in different situations. It will take no more than 15 minutes.

Possible risks and disadvantages

The survey may contain sensitive questions about your mental health. If you are experiencing severe mental health issues, this study may not be suitable for you.

Benefits of participation

Your participation will help us better understand how Ukrainian adolescent girls and young women in the UK cope and seek support for their mental health.

Confidentiality and Data Usage

Your information will be stored securely, remain anonymous, and be used solely for research purposes in compliance with UK data protection regulations.

Further Information

If you have questions or concerns, please contact Sophia Lobanov-Rostovsky at sophia.lobanov-rostovsky@lshtm.ac.uk.
